Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Futures trading involves buying and selling futures contracts, which are agreements to buy or sell an asset at a future date at an agreed-upon price.

To get started with futures trading, you need to open an account with a brokerage that offers futures trading, deposit funds, and start trading based on your strategy and market analysis.

Futures trading carries risks such as market risk, leverage risk, and liquidity risk. It's essential to understand these risks and use risk management strategies.

A futures contract is a standardized agreement to buy or sell a specific quantity of a commodity or financial instrument at a predetermined price at a specified future date.

Futures contracts obligate the holder to buy or sell the underlying asset, while options contracts give the holder the right but not the obligation to buy or sell the asset at a specified price.

Futures trading offers advantages such as high liquidity, leverage, and the ability to hedge against price movements in the underlying asset.

Margin in futures trading is a good faith deposit required to open and maintain a position. It acts as collateral to cover potential losses.

Risk management in futures trading can be achieved through strategies such as diversification, using stop-loss orders, and maintaining appropriate position sizes.

A futures broker facilitates the trading of futures contracts on behalf of clients, providing access to markets, executing orders, and offering advice and research.

Technical analysis involves studying past market data, primarily price and volume, to forecast future price movements. It helps traders make informed decisions in futures trading.
